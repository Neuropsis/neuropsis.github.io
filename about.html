About Neuropsis

Neuropsis is a resource grounded in fundamental neuroscience and built to serve both research and clinical communities. We begin with the principles of neurobiology, physiology, and biophysics—how signals emerge in individual neurons, and more importantly, how meaningful output arises only through networks and connectivity.

Our approach moves from bench to bedside. We evaluate the very tools we use to infer brain function—from cell signalling and synaptic transmission to invasive and non-invasive methods like EEG, EMG, MEG, PET, DTI, DWI, SWI, and brain stimulation (TMS, DBS, tES). We incorporate pharmacological and genetic modulators such as BDNF and APOE, and explore how behaviour, perception, and cognition can be meaningfully measured.

While clinical neurology often rests on a localist view—stroke here, deficit there—basic research reminds us that the brain rarely operates in isolation. This site embraces that tension and pushes toward a more integrated model, driven by connectionist thinking. Our goal is to make this science practical for diagnosis, clinical decision-making, and advancing the next generation of neurotechnology.
